EVENT

Legendary Beat is an annual event that is set to celebrate the art legends in Kenya. On that day we choose to appreciate the people who made a difference in the society, in a social way and have left a mark in the way in which they expressed their passions. Through their artistic works, music, books and their impact to the society.

We also aim to inform the talented youths in the art industry and all other passions related about the people who made the industry what they found it as. This will challenge them to move the industry to the next level. To make them understand that passion is driven by discipline, sacrifice and commitment. The event will be an educating platform as much as it will appreciate legends and give motivation as well.

LEGENDS

Suzzana Owiyo

Suzzana is an all-time musician whose style of music is unique and express her identity internationally. Having been born near the lake side(Nyakatch) in the city of Kisumu, she lived there for during her early ages and was introduced to music by her grandfather who played ‘Nyatiti’. She worked with a few artists as a backup vocalist for example for Sally Oyugi. In 1998, she got her first hit song ‘Sandore’ made her win the Kisima award for the most promising Female artist in 2003.She worn a number of other awards and is a legend. She also made a great memory when she sang for the late Nelson Mandela on his 90th birthday concert in London.

Joe Kadenge

Joe Kadenge (official name) is a Kenyan soccer maestro whose football magic never tarnished and is regarded as the finest footballer ever produced by the country. He started playing football at an early age when kids would roll dry banana fibers into a ball until he started playing competitively in 1957.After representing the National team for 14years, he retired and was appointed as a coach of Haram bee stars. He still remains to be a legend; “Kadenge na mpira.shuti goal!”.The commentators of the day in particular one Leonard Mambo Mbotela had the best description of Kadenge’s soccer prowess and marked the slogan as part of the glory days in Kenyan football.

Wangari Mathai

The first Woman environmentalist to win the Nobel Peace Prize in 2004.She is a conservation legend and leader. The Green Belt is the initiative she initiated in 1997.She facilitated planting of over 10,000,000 trees in Kenya, helped to defeat the plans by Kenyan president to destroy forests for the building of luxury. Wangari Mathai describe trees as “seeds of peace, seeds of democracy and seeds of respect for human rights.” She wrote a number of books: Greenbelt Movement, Unbowed, A Memoir; The challenge for Africa and replenishing the earth; Flight of the humming bird. “It is the little things citizens do. That’s what will make a difference. My little thing is planting trees” she said.

Catherine Ndereba

She is a retired marathon runner, who is known for championing most women marathons during the peak of her career.She trained while at Ngorano Secondary School, and made her international debut in 1995.As she grew older Ndereba was training to be a runner which was something she enjoyed, and it was very obvious that she was going to be pretty famous for abilities. The runners that she trained with had difficulty completing certain running courses that she could finish with ease.Catherine participated in the Boston marathon and was the first woman to win four. She has also worn championships in athletics and worn silver medals in the Olympics in 2004 and 2008.For a year from 2001 to 2002 she held the women's marathon world record, before it was broken by Paula Radcliffe.

Mzee Ojwang

He was a veteran TV comedian (Hero of evolving comedy.) his official names are Benson Wanjau. He Started acting after working as a technician at Matter Hospital and at White Rose Drycleaners. He started working while he was in his early youth after dropping out of school due to lack of finances. He Started acting in 1980 and has acted for a number of comedy series; Darubini, Vioja Mahakamani, vituko, kinyonga and vitimbi. Vitimbi is the most common to this generation, it is where most of us saw Mzee Ojwang as from the time we were young until he passed on. He played a Luo character, through his manner of speech and pronunciation, though he was from the Kikuyu ethnicity. He acted alongside ‘mama Kaya’ since the emerging of the vitimbi series until they were mistaken for being real couples.

Frank Odoi

An African brilliant cartoonist who was a Ghanaian based in Kenya. He moved to Nairobi in the 1970’s and worked here ever since. He was one of the first visual artists to be given a daily slot in Kenya’s Daily National Newspaper. During his time, he dominated the Kenyan artistic scene. He did fiction and political commentary which was featured in newspapers all over Africa and the BBC’S focus on Africa Magazine. Famously known for Golgoti Series, about a white man who comes to Africa and Akokhan, the tale of two centuries-old squabbling men which was based on the folk core of his native Ghana.” I want to be remembered for (my) comics. I’d like to sit back and create comics for both children and adults…with stories like Akokhan”, he said.
